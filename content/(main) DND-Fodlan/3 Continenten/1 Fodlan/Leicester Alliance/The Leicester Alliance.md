
The Alliance is composed of various noblemen who have grouped together for their common interests.

### History of the Alliance
#### origins
Sixty years after the Leicester region joined the Kingdom of Faerghus in 801, King Klaus I divided his territory between his three sons. One son inherited the Leicester region, but died twenty years later, and the nobles of the region refused to acknowledge his heir. Instead, house Riegan led a rebellion against the Kingdom, and secured independence for the region in 901.

#### The Almyran Wars
In 961, the forces of Almyra launched their greatest assault on Fodlan, starting in the Leicester Alliance. While it seemed that the invaders would prevail, the Adrestian Empire came to the Alliance's aid and drove back the threat. It was decided that a fortification was needed at the border, for the sake of not only the Alliance, but all of Fodlan. The Empire, the Kingdom, and the Alliance all came together to construct Fodlan's Locket, an impenetrable fortress that lies on the mountain range known as Fodlan's throat. The fortress was completed in 1101 and given to house Goneril due to their contributions in the war

#### The Riegan Scandal
In 1174, house Riegan lost its only heir with the death of Godfrey Von Riegan. With house Riegan being as powerful as it is, the Alliance was set to experience a huge shift in power. Duke Oswald Von Riegan set off to find his daughter Tiana, who had run away from her homeland many years ago. When he returned, it was not with his daughter, but with his grandson, a young man named Claude. He was announced as the new heir, quelling the original distress to introduce a new one. Many questioned the young man and his credibility as Riegan's legitimate heir.

#### The New Duke Riegan
Barely a few months before this setting takes place, Duke Oswald Von Riegan stepped down from his position in order to live out the last year or two of his life in peace. Now his grandson, Claude Von Riegan, has stepped in to lead the Alliance in his stead. This change has caused quite a commotion within the Alliance, and many are split in their opinions of the young Duke. None can deny that his tactics for both the battlefield and the roundtable are stunning to behold, and he has proven willing to throw out old traditions in order to achieve his goals. Many of the more conservative nobles, such as Gloucester, see him as a threat, while many of the more freespirited nobles, such as Goneril, see him as a welcome change.

### Aspects of the Alliance
What the Alliance lacks in history, it makes up for in personality. Each territory has its own way of expressing itself, and the clash of aesthetics makes sure that there's never a dull sight

#### Architecture
The architecture of the Alliance is reminiscent of southwestern Europe, specifically Italy, Spain, and France. Waterways make commerce easy, as well as provide hydration to crops in otherwise infertile soil. Large, extravagant manors are common among the nobility, and huge art pieces are put in public display as a show of wealth.

#### The Roundtable
While each house serves its own interests, they do have to come together to maintain peace. Five of the most prominent houses: Riegan, Gloucester, Goneril, Ordelia, and Edmund, all gather at the capital of Derdriu to discuss important subjects. Among them, Riegan is considered the semi-official leader of the Alliance, though their power is far from absolute. House Daphnel once held a seat at the roundtable, but after the house split apart, it was given to Edmund.

#### Land of Merchantry
Due to lax laws and regulations, the Alliance has enjoyed a booming economy. Small businesses and large industries alike thrive, so long as they're willing to make some backalley deals, of course... This freedom does come at the cost of having those who abuse it. Scams are not at all uncommon, and regional monopolies are fairly standard

#### Expressive Freedom
The people of Leicester highly value art and fashion, with each region having its own trends. Even commonfolk spruce up their outfits with flowers, accessories, and cheap dyes. 

#### Invaders from the East
For hundreds of years, armies and brigands from Almyra have invaded through the mountain range known as Fodlan's throat. While Fodlan's Locket keeps them in check, they still remain a persistent, though negligible, threat. Hostilities between Almyra and Leicester have actually calmed down since Duke Claude came into power. Discrimination against Almyrans is present in the Alliance, especially in the eastern side, but Almyrans can still make a living in Leicester, if they so desire.

### Noble Houses of the Alliance
#### House Riegan
Originally a cadet branch of house Blaiddyd, house Riegan led the rebellion against the Kingdom in order to form the Leicester Alliance. They serve as the leaders of the Alliance, though their influence has slowly lessened as the Alliance continues to establish its own identity. The house carries the blood of the Elite Riegan and bear his crest. They also possess the Hero's Relic Failnaught, which is passed down between Dukes.

###### Locations in the Dukedom
The Dukedom of Riegan is a prosperous region in the northern region of Leicester.
* **The City of Derdriu** is the capital of Leicester, and the seat of house Riegan's power. The Alliance Roundtable holds its meetings in Derdriu. The city is known for its factories, allowing massive production of goods traded all throughout Fodlan. It's located in the perfect spot to trade with Faerghus from across the sea, and the nearby rivers allow smaller ships to travel from the northern seas to the Airmid River and down to the Empire

#### House Gloucester
House Gloucester is often considered the second most powerful house in the Alliance. Count Gloucester is known for his outstanding reputation among the commonfolk, and his extraordinary wealth. Instead of focusing on the major centerpoint of the territory, house Gloucester has distributed attention between the entire (quite large) region. House Gloucester is descended from the Elite of the same name, and bear his Crest. They also hold the Hero's Relic Thyrsus in their possession. While the house has an esteemed reputation, it is not without its controversies. Territory disputes are common, and rumors of foul schemes; some as serious as assassination, have propagated in recent years.

###### Locations in the County
Gloucester territory encompasses most of the central Alliance. The region is known for its artisanry and culinary accomplishments. 
* **Edgaria** is the largest city within the County and is known for its unmatched agricultural infrastructure. While less extravagant than most other major cities, it boasts a huge size and a thriving community. 
* **Sauin** is a small but prominent village at the edge of Gloucester territory. The people there gain their livelihood from hunting, both for natural fauna and rare monsters that prowl the lands. The village suffered from poaching until 1174, when a group of mercenaries dealt with the issue temporarily. 
* **The Bridge of Myrddin** Does not actually reside in Gloucester territory, but longstanding deals allow Gloucester some control over its management and defenses. The bridge spans the Airmid River, connecting the Alliance to the Empire. While other bridges span the river as well, Myrddin is the only one large enough to support a full army. 

#### House Goneril
House Goneril is responsible for protecting the mountain range known as Fodlan's Throat from Almyran invaders. While a very traditional house due to its military history, it has recently taken on a more progressive stance due to a change in leadership. The people of the region have become very artistic, and are well known for their craftsmanship of all kinds of goods. As descendants of the Hero Goneril, they bear the Crest of the same name, and utilize the Hero's Relic Freikugel in their fight against Almyran bandits. The current leader of the house is Duke Holst, more commonly known as the great General Holst. Unlike normal succession of noble houses, the former Duke and Duchess are still alive and well, and help with the governance of the territory, while still enjoying early retirement. In addition, his younger sister assists with improving morale among the people of Fodlan's Locket, and occasionally helping out in battle, to surprising results.

###### Almyran prisoners
Before the reveal of Claude Von Riegan, Holst was thought to be the one to lead the Alliance in the future. As the leader of House Goneril, Holst is in charge of managing Almyran prisoners of war. Such prisoners cannot be sent home due to Almyra seeing capture as a failure punishable by death. They cannot be released into Fodlan or they will be
unable to lead normal lives. Holst, not being one to accept letting the Almyrans rot in their cells, chose to enact a temporary labor policy. This policy allows Almyrans to serve House Goneril as servants in exchange for limited freedoms and housing marginally better
than a jail cell. The policy has been a topic of great controversy, and is disliked by many, including those who advocate for and against Almyran rights. Opinions are split even among the Almyrans themselves, some of whom see this as their best option to return to a normal life, while others see it as prolonging their dishonor. Holst maintains that, despite its flaws, the policy is a net positive that allows for some degree of improvement. It's unlikely that anything will change

###### Locations in the Dukedom
The territory of house Goneril encompasses the mountainous region known as Fodlan's Throat, save for the northern edges.
* **Fodlan's Locket** is a fortress city located at the border between Fodlan and Almyra. Despite the constant raids and the newfound war, the people boast a strong morale thanks to their leadership. The people are also known for exceptional artisanry and aesthetics, with fashion being an important aspect of the region.

#### House Ordelia
The once prominent house of Ordelia has fallen into ruin over the last decade and a half. When house Hrym decided to try and split off from the Empire, house Ordelia came to their aid, and was punished for it. The Empire began taking over the territory, with the other houses of Leicester not getting involved to avoid confrontation. The Empire ceased their meddling after a few years, but the wounds they left may never fully heal. House Ordelia bears the Crests of Charon and Gloucester, though they rarely manifest as their blood ties to the original families are old and weak. All but one of Ordelia's heirs have died, and the current heiress has no intention of taking over the region. It is expected the house will soon lose its spot on the roundtable, if not its nobility entirely.

###### Locations in the Country
* **Ordelia** is the seat of power for the house, and is not a particularly desirable place to visit. When the Empire took control, it also set up outposts in the region. While their cruel experiments have concluded, some operatives still remain, and the technology they left behind is no longer concealed as much as it once was. Ordelia is now considered a "haunted city" with all of the strange happenings, and most folk stay far away when traveling the region

#### House Edmund
House Edmund is one of the more up-and-coming houses in the Alliance. When house Daphnel split and lost its seat on the roundtable, that seat was given to Edmund. Now, with Edmund allowing fair use of its harbors, among other revolutionary trade policies, Edmund has asserted itself as a force to be reckoned with. According to official records, Edmund holds no Crest in its bloodline.  The current Margrave Edmund is a renowned orator, and has secured a reputation as one of the most dangerous nobles in the Alliance because of this. He is cold and calculating, but still fair and reasonable, and is admired by his people.

###### Locations in the Margravate
Edmund controls the northernmost territories in the Alliance, occupying most of the northern coast.
* **Edmund** is the main city of the territory. In addition to maritime trade, it also possesses a thriving logging industry. Many of the best ships are made with wood from Edmund. 
* **Kupala** is a small village at the very north of Fodlan's Throat. While technically part of Edmund territory, the village considers itself independent, and Margrave Edmund respects that. Only the occasional workers or merchants with the Margrave's approval go up to the region.

#### House Daphnel
Once one of the five houses of the roundtable, house Daphnel fell from influence when the house split into two. Internal conflicts caused the western side of the territory to become house Galatea. While house Daphnel has struggled since then, it is currently on the rise again, thanks to its current leader. House Daphnel is descended from the Elite of the same name, and bears his Crest in their bloodline. They also possess the Crest of Riegan due to a previous marriage with house Riegan. Despite having these Crests in the bloodline, no one of house Daphnel has been born with a Crest since the split. The current leader of House Daphnel, Judith, has earned a reputation as a brilliant leader and powerful fighter. She's earned the nickname "The Hero of Daphnel".

###### Locations in the County
The territories of Daphnel encompass most of the western edge of the Alliance. Most of the land is undeveloped, making for prime hunting grounds
* **Daphnel** is a small City at the border between the Kingdom and the Alliance.  
* **Ailell**, also known as the valley of torment, is a large flaming crater in the north of Daphnel territory. The area was once a lush forest, but 70 years ago a blinding light descended onto the area, said to be the Goddess' wrath. The valley now stands permanently aflame, with lava flows that refuse to cool. The area is inhospitable, and those who remain there for too long fall ill. Despite this, it does serve as a meeting place for those who want to avoid unwanted attention

#### House Albrecht
House Albrecht is a minor noble family at the east of the Alliance. The house is known for its aggressive political schemes, and obsessing over their noble power. While recent Barons have attempted to reform the house's ways, the family is large and the corruption runs deep. The house holds the Crest of Riegan in its line due to marriage with the Riegan family a long time ago. In addition, the former heir to house Albrecht bore the Crest of Chevalier, seemingly from his mother, who was from the village of Kupala.

###### Locations in the Barony
House Albrecht holds very little territory, only a small region in between Goneril and Riegan territory. 
* **Albrecht** is a small city that tries to attract wealthy merchants with low tax rates. The area is known as a financial hub. The city also produces hardened bounty hunters, which are hired off to merchants and other houses.

#### House Phlegathon
A minor noble family known for being a general nuisance to the other lords. Not powerful enough to be of any significance, but with enough of a hold that they can't be removed entirely. The house has no Crest in its bloodline. The current Lord, Acheron, has earned the nickname "Weathervane" due to him constantly spinning between allegiances. He's recently been accused of selling valuable information to the Empire.

###### Locations in the Territory 
Acheron territory runs along the Airmid River
* **Phlegathon** is a city infamous for gambling and debauchery. Crime rates are some of the highest in Leicester, and lax regulations make it dangerous for workers. 
* **The Bridge of Myrddin** Falls in Phlegathon territory, though several other houses have a say in its management. Phlegathon uses the bridge as its main leverage in political matters.
